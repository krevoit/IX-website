What is an Internet Exchange?

An IX (Internet Exchange) allows networks to interconnect on an open-access ethernet fabric -- providing low-latency, high-bandwidth, cost-effectively, in a locale.

What rules are there?

Peers must have a publicly routable ASN and an IPv6 block
Peers cannot send static/default routes, CDP, OSPF, multicasting, proxy ARP, DHCP etc. Peers should only allow IPv4/IPv6 packets and regular ARP protocol.
We only support Dualstack (IPv4 and IPv6) or IPv6 only, peers must establish the session over IPv6
Prefixes must originate from your ASN
Prefixes must have a valid IRR and RPKI record in either ARIN, RIPE, APNIC, AFRINIC or LACNIC. Other registries can be added manually.

You must register your ASN with http://peeringdb.com, as most of our configurations are automatically pulled from there.
